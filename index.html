01 — About

What is Space Systems Lab?

Space Systems Lab is an open-source educational platform designed to make spacecraft engineering accessible to students, educators, and independent learners worldwide.

🛸

What Space Systems Lab Offers

Space Systems Lab combines interactive simulations, guided learning modules, and practical engineering activities to help students understand how spacecraft are designed, controlled, and operated. Every module emphasizes learning through experimentation rather than memorization.

🎯

Why Learn Spacecraft Design?

Modern space exploration is no longer limited to government agencies. Universities, startups, and student teams regularly develop CubeSats and small satellites, making spacecraft engineering an increasingly relevant field.

👩‍🚀

Who Is This For?

Designed for high school students, university learners, STEM educators, robotics teams, and anyone interested in spacecraft engineering. No prior aerospace background is required.

02 — Curriculum

Four Modules. One Mission.

01

Orbital Mechanics

Investigate how satellites move, calculate orbital parameters, and experiment with orbital transfers.

 02

Attitude Control

Explore how spacecraft maintain orientation using reaction wheels, magnetorquers, thrusters, and control algorithms.

 03

CubeSat Systems

Examine the major subsystems of a CubeSat, including power, communications, onboard computing, thermal control, and structure.

 04

Mission Design

Apply concepts from earlier modules to design a complete small-satellite mission from objectives to payload selection.
