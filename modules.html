Learning Modules

Four structured modules taking you from basic physics to full spacecraft mission design.

Module 01 In Development

Orbital Mechanics

[Placeholder] This module covers the foundational physics of how spacecraft move in orbit. Topics include Newton's law of gravitation, Kepler's three laws, types of orbits (LEO, MEO, GEO, HEO), Hohmann transfers, and delta-v budgets. By the end of this module, you'll understand how to calculate orbital parameters and plan basic maneuvers.

Module 02 In Development

Attitude Control

[Placeholder] Attitude control determines how a spacecraft orients itself in space. This module covers reference frames, Euler angles and quaternions, reaction wheels, magnetorquers, thrusters, PID controllers, and the basics of ADCS (Attitude Determination and Control Systems). You'll work through simulations showing how different actuators affect spacecraft rotation.

Module 03 In Development

CubeSat Systems

[Placeholder] CubeSats have democratized access to space. This module walks through every subsystem of a 1U/3U CubeSat — power systems (solar panels, batteries, EPS), communications (UHF/VHF, S-band), onboard computers, thermal management, and structural design. You'll learn how to read CubeSat datasheets and understand trade-off decisions.

Mission Design

[Placeholder] The capstone module. You'll apply everything learned in Modules 1–3 to design a complete small satellite mission from scratch — defining mission objectives, selecting your orbit, choosing your payload, budgeting power and mass, and writing a basic Mission Design Document (MDD).
